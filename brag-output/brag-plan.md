# Brag Plan: StyleSync AI

## What is this app?
StyleSync AI digitizes your wardrobe with AI vision, reads live weather, and hands you a match-scored outfit recommendation every morning instead of a closet you stare into.

## The angle
This is not a hype-SaaS launch. StyleSync's own brand voice is "measured, deliberate, editorial" and explicitly rejects big-metric-grid SaaS energy. So the video plays like an editorial fashion-magazine spread that happens to be a software product: serif type, sand and sage palette, a slow confident reveal, and one real, quietly damning stat carrying the whole argument. The proof is a garment getting classified in seconds and a real outfit card landing with a match score, not a bullet list of features.

## Hook (first 2-3 seconds)
The landing page's own hero headline reveal, recreated exactly: "Your wardrobe," then the italic sage line "intelligently" sliding up from below, then "curated." Corner index marks (SS.AI / 001) and the thin editorial rule are already part of the source design and should open the video.

## Key moments (the middle)
- The claim: the stats-band quote from the site, "The average wardrobe holds 77 items. Most people wear 20% of them," full-bleed on the dark background, exactly as styled on the landing page.
- The proof, part one: a garment photo gets uploaded and classified. Tag pills ("Wool", "Sage", "Tailored") land one by one, fast, showing the AI vision pipeline actually working.
- The proof, part two: cut to the real dashboard moment, the "Today's Recommendation" card: a sun weather icon, a match-score badge, and the outfit collage settling into place.

## Outro / punchline
Final CTA card from the site, recreated: "Dress with intention." on the dark background with the sage italic word, then the "Create Your Wardrobe" button and the StyleSync AI serif wordmark as the closing hold.

## User flow worth showing
1. Entry: user uploads a garment photo in the Wardrobe Studio.
2. Key action: AI vision classifies it in seconds (category, color, style tag pills appear).
3. Result: the dashboard's Today's Recommendation card resolves with a weather icon, a match score, and a styled outfit.

## Tone
- Preset: polished
- Creative direction: quiet premium editorial film. Precise, unhurried, magazine-confident. No hype language, no metric-grid flex.
- Interpretation: fewer scenes, longer holds, soft crossfades instead of hard cuts. Type does the work; motion stays restrained and deliberate rather than fast and loud.

## Format: landscape - 1920x1080
## Duration: 20s

## Visual identity (from the project)
- Background: #faf6f0 (sand), with #121210 (dark charcoal) used for the claim and outro scenes exactly as the source site alternates
- Accent: #708272 (sage), italic treatment for the emphasis word in every headline
- Text: #1c1917 on light scenes, #fcfbf9 (cream) on dark scenes
- Display font: Cormorant Garamond (serif, weight 300 to 400, italic for accent words)
- Body font: Geist Sans (uppercase, wide letter-spacing, small size, used for eyebrows/labels/CTAs)
- Strongest visual element: the hero's clip-and-reveal headline lines with the corner index marks (SS.AI / 001) and the thin vertical/horizontal editorial rules

## Share copy (draft)
StyleSync AI turns your closet into a searchable wardrobe and hands you one weather-smart outfit each morning. Dress with intention, not decision fatigue.

## Audio direction
- Role: warm, restrained bed with sparse professional accents
- Music: happy-beats-business-moves-vol-12-by-ende-dot-app.mp3 (steady, clean, 109.96 BPM, matches "polished")
- Music treatment: start at 0s under the hook at low volume (0.3), hold steady through the middle, gentle swell into the outro card, fade out over the last 1s
- Music cue guidance: preset read from assets/music/cues/happy-beats-business-moves-vol-12-by-ende-dot-app.music-cues.json. Strong cues in window: 8.74s (0.99), 13.11s (0.98), 17.47s (0.99), 18.56s (0.99), 22.93s outside 20s window so not used. Target the tag-pill sequence start near 8.74s and the outro card entrance near 17.47-18.56s. Beat-grid points near 6.56s and 15.29s are available for smaller entrance nudges. Treat all of these as bias, not a lock: readability and the storyboard's own holds win any conflict.
- Audio-reactive treatment: subtle. Sage hero glow and the outfit card's presence may breathe slightly with music RMS. No waveform or equalizer visuals.
- SFX posture: minimal but present, 2-3 cues total, nothing aggressive
- Audio-coupled moments: tag pills landing one by one (soft drop per pill, thin out after the first two), the outfit card resolving (one gentle bell/bong accent), the outro CTA appearing
- Restraint rule: no dense per-beat SFX, no glitch or impact-heavy families, no cue louder than the music bed

## Storyboard

### Scene 1 — Hook - Headline — 3.5s
Sand background (#faf6f0). Corner marks "SS.AI" (top-left) and "001" (bottom-right) fade in first. Thin horizontal rule slides in from the left. Headline reveals line by line: "Your wardrobe," (serif, dark), then "intelligently" (serif italic, sage, indented), then "curated." (serif, dark). Eyebrow line "AI-Powered Wardrobe Intelligence" sits above in small caps sage text.
Sequential/interaction: yes, the three headline lines clip-reveal upward one after another, matching the source site's own stagger.
Audio intent: quiet confidence, the video introducing itself without announcing it
Audio-coupled idea: none, let the reveal carry in silence apart from the music bed entering
Music: bed fades in under this scene at low volume
Transition mood: soft crossfade → Scene 2

### Scene 2 — The claim — 3.5s
Cut to dark background (#121210). Centered serif quote, large: "The average wardrobe holds 77 items." then a beat, then the italic sage line "Most people wear 20% of them." appears as its own emphasis.
Sequential/interaction: yes, the second sentence arrives about 1s after the first, holding the full quote on screen for the remainder of the scene
Audio intent: the one line that earns a reaction, let it land and sit
Audio-coupled idea: none, no SFX on the text itself, protect the read
Music: steady under the quote, no swell yet
Transition mood: soft crossfade → Scene 3

### Scene 3 — The product, in use — 8s
Sand background. Left/top: a garment photo card appears (a folded knit, styled like the wardrobe grid). Tag pills land one by one beside it: "Wool", "Sage", "Tailored" (about 0.5-0.6s apart, each settling before the next). Around 4.5s, cut/crossfade to the real dashboard moment: the "Today's Recommendation" card, sun weather icon, a match-score badge (e.g. "94% Match"), and the outfit collage image settling into place. "Generate Looks" sparkle button visible as a small detail.
Sequential/interaction: yes, tag pills arrive one by one (garment classification), then the recommendation card's weather icon, score badge, and collage arrive in that order as a second sequence
Audio intent: this is the proof, it should feel alive and real, not like a slide
Audio-coupled idea: soft drop sound on the first tag pill only (thin out for the rest), one gentle bell/bong accent when the match-score badge lands
Music: bed continues steady, slight presence swell as the recommendation card resolves
Transition mood: soft crossfade for the internal cut, soft crossfade → Scene 4

### Scene 4 — Outro — 5s
Cut to dark background (#121210), matching the site's final CTA section. Large serif headline: "Dress with" then italic sage "intention." beneath it. Below that, the cream CTA pill "Create Your Wardrobe" fades up. Final hold: the StyleSync AI serif wordmark, small and centered, letter-spaced, as the very last frame.
Sequential/interaction: yes, headline then CTA button then wordmark arrive as three restrained beats, each held long enough to read
Audio intent: a quiet, settled close, not a big finish
Audio-coupled idea: one soft accent (interface/bong_001 or impactSoft_medium) as the CTA button appears, nothing on the wordmark
Music: gentle swell under the headline, then fade out over the final second as the wordmark holds
Transition mood: hold → end

**Music mood for this video:** polished, steady, restrained corporate-adjacent bed (vol-12)
